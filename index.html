Seven Wonders

CHICHEN ITZA | Mexico

El Castillo, or the Temple of Kukulcan, a Mesoamerican pyramid in Chichen Itza, Mexico, built by the Maya in the 9th-12th centuries, honors the Feathered Serpent deity. With 91 steps on each of its four sides, the pyramid's unique design creates a shadow play during equinoxes, resembling a crawling serpent, and stands 24 meters tall with a 55.3-meter square base.

CHRIST REDEEMER | Brazil

Christ the Redeemer, an iconic Art Deco statue in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, stands 30 meters tall on Corcovado mountain, weighing 635 metric tons. Built from 1922 to 1931, it symbolizes Christianity globally and is a New Seven Wonders of the World.

COLOSSEUM | Italy

The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is a colossal oval amphitheatre in Rome, Italy, built by the Flavian dynasty in AD 80. Once hosting gladiatorial contests, it remains an iconic symbol of Imperial Rome, attracting tourists and connecting to the Roman Catholic Church traditions.

TAJ MAHAL | India

The Taj Mahal, an ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1632 for his wife Mumtaz Mahal, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of Mughal architecture. Described as "the tear-drop on the cheek of time," it attracts millions of visitors annually and was recognized as one of the New7Wonders of the World in 2007.

GREAT WALL | China

The Great Wall of China, spanning 15 provinces, is a historic fortification built to defend against Eurasian Steppe nomadic invasions. Constructed over centuries, the majority from the Ming Dynasty, it stretches approximately 13,171 miles, serving defensive, economic, and regulatory purposes along its iconic path.

MACHU PICCHU | Peru

Machu Picchu, a 15th-century Inca citadel, perched at 2,430 meters above sea level in Peru, was likely built for Inca emperor Pachacuti and is a renowned icon of Inca civilization. Often termed the "Lost City of the Incas," it gained global recognition in 1911 and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

PETRA | Jordan

Petra, an ancient city in Jordan known for its impressive rock-cut architecture and water systems, was established around 312 BC by the Nabataeans. A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New7Wonders of the World, Petra's rose-colored structures make it a symbol of Jordan and a top tourist attraction.
